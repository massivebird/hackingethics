The world is full of constantly-evolving technology and networks that span the globe. This creates a growing responsibility for those who control and oversee our data. Should these people betray our trust or take advantage of their power, the consequences would be dire. As more and more devices and services integrate themselves into the globalized system, those repercussions grow ever more dangerous. We must trust ourselves and those around us to keep our information and identities safe.

This website describes what hacking is, what types of hackers exist, and the consequences / benefits of legal and illegal use of hacking skills. It is crucial that those who learn how to keep our data safe do not choose to become criminals, using the same skills that others use for good.

It is crucial that those with potentially dangerous skillsets are aware of their authority over the modern world, lest they abuse it.

[hak-ing]

a. to modify (a computer program or electronic device) or write (a program) in a skillful or clever way

b. to circumvent security and break into (a network, computer, file, etc.), usually with malicious intent

What is white hat hacking?

White hat hackers utilize their skills and knowledge in a safe environment. These are the specialists who make a career out of their otherwise alarming skillsets. Companies and other entities hire them (often through generous payment) to find holes in data bases and security systems, report the issues, and eliminate the vulnerabilities. The mutual arrangement between the hacker and the client is what makes their work perfectly legal and beneficial to both parties.

Large corportations and government bodies often run "bug bounties" where both amateur and experienced hackers are invited to find holes and weaknesses within a designated site or domain. If the rules are followed, then these people may be subsequently rewarded for their efforts. Their goal is to protect confidential and personal information from getting in the hands of rogue hackers.

What is black hat hacking?

Black hat hackers possess the same skills as white hats. However, their work is done not to secure systems, but to take advantage of them. Often with the goals of financial gain, political attention, or sabotage, black hats seek control over entire systems and databases, attempting to illegally steal and sell personal information, forcefully promote their ideals, and damage the reputation of large entities.

Similarly, grey hat hackers aim to surreptitiously identify vulnerabilities within various websites and systems. However, instead of immediately stealing information, they contact the server owner or entity, demanding money for the unspecified issue. Should the offer be denied, the hackers may threaten to leak the vulnerability or deal damage to the system. Due to the lack of a mutual agreement or pre-requested permission, this is highly illegal.

The cyberspace is not all fun and games.

The internet is no longer a simple resource for information, but a tool, a utility, and a keepsafe for our valuables. Anyone can choose to store nothing at all - or company records, credit card numbers, and social security numbers. That being said, accessing foreign computer systems without authorization is a crime punishable by law. The cyberspace holds belongings just as important as those in the real world - and trespassers are promptly charged.

Computer crimes can range anywhere from identity theft, piracy, spreading malware or viruses, and even espionage. In the State of Michigan, a large variety of actions are considered cybercrimes. These can go on your legal record as either misdemeanors or felonies depending on how much money is involved in the crime itself. Fines and jail time are also possible consequences and can vary in intensity.
