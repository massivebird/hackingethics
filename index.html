Hacking Ethics

with Garrett Drake

"Technology is nothing. What's important is that you have a faith in people, that they're basically good and smart, and if you give them tools, they'll do wonderful things with them."
- Steve Jobs

The world is full of constantly-evolving technology and networks that span the globe. This creates a growing responsibility for those who control and oversee our data. Should those people betray our trust or take advantage of their power, the consequences would be dire. As more and more devices and services integrate themselves into the globalized system, those repercussions grow ever more dangerous. We must trust ourselves and those around us to keep our information and identities safe.

This website describes what hacking is, what types of hackers exist, and the consequences / benefits of legal and illegal use of hacking skills. It is crucial that those who learn how to keep our data safe do not choose to become criminals, using the same skills that others use for good.

[hak-ing]

a. to modify (a computer program or electronic device) or write (a program) in a skillful or clever way

b. to circumvent security and break into (a network, computer, file, etc.), usually with malicious intent
